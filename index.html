Fake Port-In Fraud Detection — Dual Signal Model
Fraudulent retailers are caught by two independent signals: (1) 90d+ threshold gaming — customers pass the 60-day KB threshold via a coached top-up then immediately exit, visible as high 60d-stay but near-zero 90d+ retention; (2) KB exceeds total revenue — Lyca paid more in kickbacks than it earned in subscription revenue from that retailer's customers, a direct financial loss. Any retailer triggering both signals should be considered for immediate contract termination.

KB Exceeds Revenue — Direct Financial Loss
These 18 retailers generated less total revenue (first bundle + renewals) than Lyca paid them in kickback incentives. Even before counting network costs and overhead, Lyca lost money on every port-in these retailers brought. This is a standalone fraud signal independent of retention metrics — a retailer could have decent 90d retention but still be KB-farming if they onboard low-ARPU customers.

Why KB/Revenue matters: If a retailer earns €100 in first bundles + renewals but receives €150 in kickbacks, Lyca has a net −€50 on that retailer before any operating costs. This directly measures whether the incentive program is being gamed — legitimate retailers should generate multiple times their KB in lifetime revenue.   |   Why 90d+ beats 60d: KB is unlocked at >60 days. Fraudsters ensure one top-up around day 50 to trigger KB, then customers exit. active_grt_90 captures genuine long-term subscribers that no gaming strategy can fake cheaply.
