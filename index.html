💸
Total Incentive (KB+GARA) Exceeds Revenue — Direct Financial Loss
These 36 retailers generated less total revenue (first bundle + renewals) than Lyca paid them in combined KB + GARA incentives. GARA is a per-activation bonus that does not require retention — meaning a retailer can receive GARA on every port-in regardless of whether customers stay. This makes GARA particularly susceptible to volume gaming: recruit any subscriber, collect GARA, accept churn. With GARA included, the number of loss-making retailers doubled from 18 to 36.

Why GARA amplifies fraud risk: Unlike KB (which requires 60-day retention to unlock), GARA is a port-in activation bonus. A retailer can receive GARA on every single activation — including ones that churn within days. This means a fraudulent retailer gets both GARA and KB on every coached port-in, increasing their incentive-per-port ratio and making the economics of fraud more attractive. GARA on its own is not evidence of fraud, but combined with poor retention it significantly worsens the financial impact.  |  Why 90d+ beats 60d: KB unlocks at >60 days. Fraudsters coach one top-up ~day 50–55 to pass the threshold then exit. active_grt_90 catches genuine long-term subscribers no gaming strategy can fake cheaply.
